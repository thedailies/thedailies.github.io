Word of the Day

Fell (n./v./adj., FELL)

Triple word score! Not quite, but still—"fell" has multiple meanings. In addition to being the past tense of fall, it can also mean timber that was cut down in a season, the hide of an animal, a rocky hill, inhumanly cruel/fierce, or lethal. So yes, you could have a sentence of "The fell fell fell down the fell." It would be unclear but, at that point, your biggest problem is evil trees and not grammar.

Link of the Day

Slow motion lightning

A stormchaser in South Dakota snapped some great slow-motion footage of a lightning storm earlier this month. As usual, HD and full screen is best.

I'm not sure why the lightning video is more mesmerizing than other nature videos but there's something incredibly cool about watching the lightning fan out, find the ground, and then travel the path repeatedly. And the speed of the wiper blade gives you an idea how slowed-down this video is (2,000 fps). Very cool.
