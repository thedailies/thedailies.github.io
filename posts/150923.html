Word of the Day

Amanuensis (n., a-MAN-you-ENN-siss)

Someone who is employed to take dictation or copy manuscripts. This word comes from the Latin for "(slave) at handwriting," which is undoubtedly how some monks felt when they were asked to copy yet another book. Enough already, Cardinal Business XXVIII! It can wait!
