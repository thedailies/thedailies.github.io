Word of the Day

Hagiography (n., HAGE-ee-OGG-ruh-fee)

Writing about the lives of saints, especially a biography of one. Also (and more common), writing a biography of a normal person so that they appear to be a saint. So whether you're writing about Francis of Assisi or making Kim Jong Un seem like him, you're a hagiographer.

Link of the Day

Matthias Pliessnig's steam-twisted wood benches

Matthias Pliessnig has been bending wood for more than ten years. He started in college, when he realized that a boat he was building worked well as a bench. Pleissnig developed a technique that allows him to steam wood for 10 minutes, making it malleable for about 30 seconds. Pleissnig's benches are wildly curved, as his Instagram feed shows:
