Link of the Day

16th-century miniature boxwood carvings

This is some next-level, intricate craftsmanship:

Colossal has more information about where these things came from, although it is a bit murky:

It is believed that the entire body of work was created during a 30-year window between 1500 and 1530, somewhere in Flanders or the Netherlands.

The tiny altarpieces, rosaries, and prayer beads are each produced from a single boxwood fragment, incorporating pins smaller than a grass seed that hold the pieces together.

This video helps to show how these were constructed.
