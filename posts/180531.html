Link of the Day

ADA art installation

Ok, this just looks like a ton of fun:

ADA is a giant helium-filled balloon with charcoal spikes that museum visitors can interact with and use to draw. This video is from its weeklong installation at a museum in Munich, Germany. The artist, Karina Smigla-Bobinski, says:

“The globe put in action fabricates a composition of lines and points, which remain incalculable in their intensity, expression, and form however hard the visitor tries to control ADA, to drive her, to domesticate her. Whatever he tries out, he would notice very soon, that ADA is an independent performer, studding the originally white walls with drawings and signs.”

You can learn more about ADA and Smigla-Bobinski's other works at her website.
